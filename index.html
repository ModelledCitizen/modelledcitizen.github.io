About Me

David Azizi is a product manager and data scientist. He currently leads the data product for The Context, a small startup working on big questions. David previously worked as a Junior Survey Data Analyst at Change Research, and managed the Hopkins Research Lab at the University of Pennsylvania where he contributed to and organized an array of research projects including a textual analysis of a large corpus of state party platforms and a conjoint analysis of voter preferences regarding racism and misogyny. David holds a B.A. in Political Science from the University of Pennsylvania, where he minored in Survey Research and Data Analytics. He also took courses at the Fox School of Business at Temple University, where he studied Management Information Systems and Data Science. While a student at Penn, he worked with the Program on Opinion Research and Election Studies, the NBC Elections Unit, and Prof. Marc Meredith on a project to update the process used to weight the National Election Pool exit poll. At Hopkins Lab, David worked alongside Prof. Daniel Hopkins, applying data science to political science research. As a pollster at Change Research, David drafted, fielded, and analyzed surveys for campaigns across the country. He also analyzed the effect of COVID-19 on voter preferences before and after the November 2020 election. Though his work has previously been focused on politics, David is a generalist and is comfortable applying statistical techniques and machine learning to any kind of data — even bioinformatics. David has since developed competency managing the development of software products working in a small startup environment.
